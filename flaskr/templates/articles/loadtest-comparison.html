The internet performance test tool comparisons

If you actually want a proper analysis of some performance test tools: click here

I've been doing performance testing for about 7 years now and I typically default to my preferred load test tool: Jmeter. It's free, runs ok, and has a ton of community support around it when it comes to things such as reporting. But the other day I was curious what else is out there for getting your performance test needs sorted and I did what any modern internet user does and that's to Google it.

The results were incredibly dissapointing. Of course it's Google so the first things displayed are the ads, but scrolling down you end up with the typical 'top # tools here!'. Then clicking on the first one hosted by guru99.com, it lists a bunch of tools without explaining what you should be looking for and then picks some paid service called LoadNinja (hosted by Smarbear, the ones behind SoapUI ... ew). I suspect a nice brown enveloper was included as part of the top load test tools there.

Now as a bit of fun, lets delve down into the 2 tools I know very well, Jmeter and Loadrunner.

Starting off it looks pretty ok. Free tool not needing state-of-the-art infrastructure, I mean sure I guess, but it's free so that's the most important thing here. Portable? Sure. Less scripting? Arguable, but nothing wrong there. Support Tomcat collectors? Oh this I didn't know about, normally when it comes to reporting I use whatever the infrastructure team has got setup or worst case run nmon/perfmon and get my metrics that way. Neat that Jmeter supports it.

Well that's a bit embarrasing... Moving on!

Looking at our next one down is Loadrunner and the header text is fairly accurate, it does have very nice reports. But under features...

Lowers the cost of load testing? In what world!? It jacks those prices skyhigh where licenses cost in the thousands. Loadrunner is all about selling its bells and whisles to large corporations who can splash a bit of cash or the HP/Microfocus sales team have worked their silver tongues on middle management. Most of the performance testing taking place is at a browser level and yes Loadrunner can do that, but there's no need to pay thousands for that privilege.

Then the following features ... I mean geez. Every other point listed is something that literally any other performance test tool will do. 'Reduces the risk of deploying systems', 'lowers hardware costs', 'utilization tracking'

It reads like keyword stuffing

To not even mention the actual benefits of Loadrunner such as the range of protocols supported or the auto correlation, to me appears as if the writer has never used Loadrunner and basically gave up past the 5th tool listed.

Then there's this which I found amusing

Which protocols does Loadrunner support? ALL OF THEM. All the protocols. HTTP? Yes. SMTP? Yes. USB? Well it says all of them so I guess yes!

Need to performance test your microprocessors? Well Loadrunner has you covered ... apparently
